Mahatma Gandhi: The Father of the Nation

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (1869–1948) was the primary leader of India's independence movement and a pioneer of the philosophy of non-violence.

1. Key Philosophical Pillars

  • Satyagraha (Truth-Force): A method of non-violent resistance to injustice.
  • Ahimsa: Absolute non-violence in thought, word, and deed.
  • Sarvodaya: The "upliftment of all," focusing on the welfare of the poorest.
  • Swadeshi: The use of locally made goods to promote self-reliance (symbolized by the Charkha or spinning wheel).

2. Major Movements & Milestones

Gandhi's leadership transformed the Indian National Congress into a mass movement.

Event Year Significance
South Africa Stay 1893–1914 Developed his first tools of non-violent resistance against racial laws.
Champaran Satyagraha 1917 His first major victory in India, fighting for indigo farmers.
Non-Cooperation Movement 1920–1922 Aimed for Swaraj (self-rule) by boycotting British institutions.
Dandi March (Salt March) 1930 A 24-day march to protest the British salt tax; a turning point in the struggle.
Quit India Movement 1942 Issued the famous call to "Do or Die" for immediate independence.

3. Social Reforms

Gandhi wasn't just a political leader; he was a social revolutionary.

  • Untouchability: He fought against the caste system, calling the oppressed classes Harijans ("Children of God").
  • Communal Unity: He fasted repeatedly to stop violence between Hindus and Muslims.
  • Education: Proposed the Nai Talim system, emphasizing craft-based education.

4. Legacy

  • Assassination: He was killed on January 30, 1948, by Nathuram Godse.
  • Global Impact: His methods inspired civil rights leaders worldwide, including Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.
  • International Day of Non-Violence: Observed annually on his birthday, October 2nd.
